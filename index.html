Good uses

  • Suggest a realistic improvement to student life.
  • Share a concern that students are talking about.
  • Give praise for a staff member, program, event, club, team, or student group.
  • Offer an idea Student Council could actually work on.

What this is

A student leadership tool

This helps Alexa and Student Council understand student concerns, ideas, and patterns instead of relying on rumors or one-off hallway conversations.

A way to organize themes

Submissions may be sorted into topics like facilities, lunch, events, communication, academics, school culture, praise, or admin-directed concerns.

A bridge to follow-up

When appropriate, Alexa may bring themes or cleaned-up summaries to Student Council and school administration. Not every submission can become an action item.

Before you submit

This is not an emergency or safety reporting system. Do not use this form for immediate danger, threats, self-harm concerns, bullying reports, harassment, discrimination, abuse, or anything that needs urgent adult action. Contact a trusted adult, school administrator, security, Safe2Tell, or the official APS reporting process instead.

  • Use your APS account. The form is not anonymous.
  • Be specific about the issue and what change you want to see.
  • Keep it respectful, even when you are frustrated.
  • Do not submit threats, harassment, discrimination, fake reports, or abusive messages.
  • Inappropriate submissions may be reported to school administration.

What happens after you submit?

Alexa reviews new submissions

Messages are sorted by topic, urgency, and whether they are for Student Council, administration, or both.

Common themes are summarized

Repeated issues and strong suggestions may be summarized without turning the form into a public complaint wall.

Appropriate items are raised

Student Council items can become projects. Admin-directed items can be brought to weekly admin conversations when appropriate.

Serious issues go to adults

Anything threatening, unsafe, abusive, or inappropriate may be escalated instead of handled as ordinary student feedback.

Important note

Help Me Alexa is a student leadership project designed to collect and organize student voice. It does not replace official school, district, safety, discipline, discrimination, harassment, or emergency reporting channels. Submissions may be paraphrased or summarized before being shared with Student Council or administration.
